**Steve's synthesis (2026-05-04 16:15 PDT, after skimming the research doc):**

- This is **declarative vs imperative** again, closely related to **convergent vs congruent**. Recursive nesting is declarative / convergent (each layer is a self-contained statement of fact; the receiver converges to a meaning regardless of evaluation order; idempotent re-application). Ordered frame sequences are imperative / congruent (each frame mutates receiver state; the sequence IS the meaning; re-application out of order produces a different result or fails). The research finding aligns: declarative/convergent aged well at the envelope layer; imperative/congruent (Noise, QUIC, TLS handshakes) aged well at the handshake layer where the state machine is the point.
- **A sequence of messages is a command or event stream**, not the envelope of a single message. The "promise stack" the bot invented in turn 2 conflates two different things: the structure of *one message* (declarative, nested) and the structure of *a stream of messages over time* (imperative, ordered). Both exist; both are useful; neither subsumes the other. Frame-sequence transports (Noise, QUIC) are streams of events carrying payloads that are themselves envelopes.
- **pCID should be able to specify the organization of the enclosed data** -- nested set, ordered set, unordered multiset, or other. There are applications for all of them. The pcid identifies the *interpretation rules* for the payload, and "how is this payload organized?" is exactly the kind of thing those rules specify. A pcid for `grid-message-v1` says "payload is bytes, optionally re-parsable as another `grid([pcid', payload'])`" -- declarative recursion. A pcid for `event-stream-v1` says "payload is an ordered sequence of nested messages, processed in order, with stream semantics." Both could ride the same outer envelope shape `grid([pcid, payload])` *if* that hypothesis holds; outer-envelope shape is itself a specimen the wire-lab is supposed to evaluate. **One candidate envelope, multiple organizational disciplines, all selectable by pcid.**
- This collapses the apparent contradiction between "promise stack is a category error" (research finding) and "but Steve clearly wanted some layered semantics" (turn-2 reading). Both are right: the candidate outer envelope `grid([pcid, payload])` is declarative recursion; the organization of any given payload can be nested-set, ordered-set, unordered-multiset, or anything else, and the pcid names which. A "promise stack" pcid can exist as one specific organizational discipline, alongside a "promise nest" pcid, alongside a "promise event-stream" pcid, etc.
- TE-1's "promise stack" was not wrong as a *concept*; it was wrong as a *position*. It's a candidate pcid (one specific organizational discipline available to handlers), not the envelope structure itself. This is the resolution path for UT-156.b (TE-1 miscategorization).
